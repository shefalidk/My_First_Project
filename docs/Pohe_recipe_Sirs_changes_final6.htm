Maharashtrian Kande-Pohe Recipe

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 


Kande-Pohe/Poha (commonly called as Pohe) is a much-loved Indian breakfast with its origin in Maharashtra. It is a quick, easy and tasty recipe with high nutritional value.

In Maharashtra, the recipe is named after the main ingredients:

Onions (Kande) and Pohe(raw pohe)

Pohe is flattened/beaten rice. It is known by different names in different regional languages of India such as Pohe (in Marathi), Attukulu (in Telugu), Aval (in Tamil, Malayalam), Chira (in Bengali) & so on.

There are 2 types of raw pohe:

1.     Thick Pohe (Jaad Pohe)

2.     Thin Pohe (Paatal Pohe)

 

For this recipe, we will use Thick Pohe.

(Thin Pohe either break or form lumps when soaked in water).

Recipe Ingredients (for 4 servings):

Figure 3: Ingredients

 

      Thick pohe 4 cups

      Finely chopped onions (medium size) 2 cups

      Water 1 cup

      Sugar 2 teaspoons

      Salt 4 teaspoons (or as per taste)

      Oil 2 tablespoons

      Peanuts 1 cup

      Green chillies (medium size) 2

      Curry leaves 8-10

      Cumin seeds 1 teaspoon

      Mustard seeds 1 teaspoon

      Turmeric powder 2 teaspoons

 

For garnishing:

      Finely chopped fresh coriander leaves

      Scraped wet coconut

      Barik(thin) crispy yellow sev

      Chopped tomatoes cup

      Sliced lemon 1

 

Caution :

Avoid the use of green chillies & peanuts if, one wants to cook pohe for children, pregnant lady or someone with Severe Acidity.

(Substitute green chillies with a pinch of red chilli powder and peanuts with potatoes/peas)

 

Preparation Before Cooking:

 

For Soaking pohe

1.     Take pohe in a container/strainer (as shown in figure 4).

2.     Remove any unwanted food particles, if any.

3.     Pour water slowly with the cup over the entire pohe in a circular manner. None of the portions should be left dry as it will make the recipe to taste dry. If one tries to compensate the dryness later by pouring more oil, then it would be tasted oily.

(Note: Avoid holding the container with pohe under running water from tap especially if you are not using a strainer. It may lead to excess

water being soaked rapidly by pohe making them sticky & forming lumps).

 

4.     Drain excess water if any (as shown in figure 5) and keep the pohe aside for 10 minutes.

 

5.     Mix well the soaked pohe with hands. Try to smash little amount of pohe with fingers; they should get easily smashed. If they dont, then sprinkle more water on the top surface to make it moister & softer.

 

6.     Sprinkle sugar and salt on top of the soaked pohe (as shown in figure 6).

 

 

 

For Tadka

 

1.     Chop green chillies with more significant sections.

(Note: Avoid finely chopped chillies, they cant be easily removed).

 

2.     Saut peanuts as follows:

1.  Place pan on medium flame.

2.  Add oil into it. Let the oil turn medium hot.

3.  Add peanuts into it and shallow fry till it turns light brown (as shown in figure 7).

 

 

 

 

 

Cooking Steps:

 

Step 1: Preparing Tadka

 

1.     Place kadhai (pan) on medium flame & put 2 tablespoons of oil in it.

2.     Add mustard seeds into the oil.

3.     Let the mustard seeds pop up making a sizzling sound.

This indicates the correct temperature of oil for Tadka.

4.     Add cumin seeds, curry leaves and green chillies to the above.

hot oil and let them sizzle (as shown in figure 8).

 

Step 2: Sauting chopped onions

 

1.     Put chopped onions in the above sizzling mixture.

2.     Saut till it turns light brown (as shown in figure 9).

 

Step 3: Adding pohe to Tadka

 

1.     Add turmeric powder to Tadka of step 2 (as shown in figure 9).

2.     Put soaked pohe (shown in figure 6) into Tadka.

3.     Add sauted peanuts (shown in figure 7) on the top of pohe.

4.     Mix the whole mixture properly till the entire pohe turns yellow.

5.     Place a lid over the mixture for 5 minutes for steam generation and then switch the flame off.

 

Step 4: Garnishing and serving the dish

1.     Put the cooked pohe in the dish.

2.     Garnish to decorate the dish as well as to add more taste.

3.     Serve hot with sliced lemon.

(Note: Lemon/curd helps the human body to absorb the iron from pohe easily).

Health benefits :

 

       Pohe is considered a good meal for diabetics as it controls Blood Sugar Levels.

 

       A Good Probiotic :

Raw pohe has undergone fermentation process and hence retains

the microbial flora from the partially digested carbs and proteins, which is healthy

for the gut.

 

       A Good Source Of Carbohydrates:

Poha is made up of 76.9 per cent of carbohydrates and about 23 per cent of fats.

It is rich in fibre and therefore make one feel full for a longer time.

 

       Rich in iron source.
