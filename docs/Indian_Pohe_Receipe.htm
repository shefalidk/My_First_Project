Indian Pohe Receipe

Kande-Pohe/Poha (commonly called as Pohe) is much-loved Indian breakfast originated from Maharashtra
It is a quick, easy,and tasty recipe with nutritious values(Refer health benefits chart in above figure).

It is named in Marathi after the main ingredients used for its preparation: Onions(Kande) & Raw Pohe(Pohe). Raw Pohe is Flattened rice/Beaten rice.
It has different names in our regional languages as Pohe (in Marathi), Attukulu (in Telugu), Aval (in Tamil, Malayalam) & so on.

There are two types of Raw Pohe:

  • Thick Pohe (Jaad Pohe)
  • Thin Pohe (Paatal Pohe)

Note :We will be using 'Thick Pohe' for making this Recipe.
('Thin Pohe' either breaks or form lumps when soaked in water.)

Total time required for Recipe (for 4 servings):

ProcedureTime Required
Soaking10 minutes
Preparations10 minutes
Cooking10 minutes
Total30 minutes

Ingredients (for 4 servings):

IngredientsQuantity
Thick Pohe 4 cups
Sugar1 teaspoon
Salt2 teaspoons(As per taste)
Finely Chopped Onions 2 cups
Water1 cup (Refer Soaking Pohe)
Oil 4 tablespoons
Raw Peanuts1 cup
Green chillies (small size) 2 (As per spicy level required)s
Cumin Seeds 4-5
Mustard seeds 1 teaspoon
Curry leaves 1 teaspoon
Turmeric Powder2 teaspoons

For garnishing:

  • Finely Chopped fresh coriander leaves
  • Scraped dry/wet coconut
  • Thin crispy yellow Shev
  • Chopped Tomato
  • One Sliced lemon

Caution : Avoid use of green chillies & peanuts if cooking for children or someone having Severe Acidity.
(Substitute green chillies with a pinch of red chilli powder and peanuts with potatoes/peas)

Preparations Before Cooking:

Soaking Process:

  1. Take Raw Pohe in one container/strainer(as shown in figure 4).
  2. Remove any unwanted food particles if any.
  3. Pour water slowly with the cup over entire Pohe in a circular fashion.
    No portion should be left dry as it will make the overall recipe taste dry.
    If one tries to compensate the dryness later with pouring more oil, the recipe turns up oily
    (Note: Avoid holding the container with Pohe under running water from tap
    especially if you are not using a strainer. It may lead to excess water being soaked rapidly by Pohe,
    making them sticky and forming lumps).
  4. Drain excess water if any (as shown in figure 5) and keep Pohe aside for 10 minutes.
  5. After 10 minutes toss the soaked Pohe with hands. Try to crush little amount of Pohe with fingers;
    they should easily get crushed. If didn’t then sprinkle more water on the top surface to make moister and softer.
  6. Sprinkle sugar and salt on top of properly soaked Pohe (as shown in figure 6).

Preparing for Tadka:

  • Chop green chillies with bigger sections.
    (Note: Avoid finely chopped chillies, they can’t be easily removed if required.)
  • Sauté peanuts:
    1. Place pan on medium flame.
    2. Add oil into it. Let the oil turn medium hot.
    3. Add peanuts into it and shallow fry till it turns light brown(as shown in figure 7).

    • Preparing for Garnishing:

  • Finely chop coriander leaves and tomatoes.
  • Scrape coconut.
  • Place barik shev in a small bowl.

Cooking Steps:

Step 1: Preparing Tadka
  1. Place kadhai(pan) on medium flame and add two tablespoons of oil in it.
  2. Add mustard seeds into oil.
  3. Let mustard seeds pop up making a sizzling sound indicating the correct
    temperature of oil for Tadka.
  4. Add Cumin seeds, curry leaves and green chillies to above hot oil.
    and let them sizzle (as shown in figure 8).

    5. Step 2: Sauté Chopped Onions
    1. Put chopped onions in the above sizzling mixture.
    2. Sauté till it turns light brown(as shown in figure 9).

    Step 3: Adding Pohe to Tadka
    1. Add turmeric powder to Tadka of step 2 (as shown in figure 9).
    2. Put soaked Pohe shown in figure 6 into Tadka.
    3. Add sautéd peanuts (shown in figure 7) on the top of Pohe.
    4. Mix the whole mixture properly till entire Pohe turns yellow.
    5. Place Lid over the mixture for 5 minutes for steam generation
      and then switch off the flame.

      6. Step 4: Garnishing and serving the dish
      Put Pohe in the dish, garnish and serve hot with sliced lemon
      (Helath Tip: lemon/curd helps absorb iron in Pohe.)

Traditional value of Pohe:


There is a tradition in Maharashtra that
when a boy goes to a girl’s house for selecting her for marriage,
the girl’s family offers him “Kande-Pohe” handmade by the girl.
If the recipe is perfect and the boy likes it, he says
“Mulgi Pasant Ahe” ( He has liked the girl as she can cook well)
